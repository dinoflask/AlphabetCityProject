What is this?

This is the Alphabet City Project, an interactive digital art piece celebrating the 10-year anniversary of Alphabet City.

What does the Glyph represent?

The glyph is the silhouette of क (ka) — The first consonant of the Devanagari alphabet (equivalent to the letter K). Devanagari is a left-to-right abugida writing system used primarily in the Indian subcontinent. It is the fourth most widely adopted script and serves as the primary alphabet for over 120 languages, most notably Hindi, Marathi, Nepali, and Sanskrit.

How can I share a story?

We also take stories by text, email, or handwritten notes at the City Of Asylum bookstore.
